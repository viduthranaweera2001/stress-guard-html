Integrated Multimodal System for Academic Stress Assessment and Management

AI-Powered Voice Analysis & Real-time Stress Detection for Sri Lankan University Students
Learn More
Project Scope

Literature Survey

71.2% of Sri Lankan university students experience moderate to severe academic stress levels

Academic stress affects 71.2% of Sri Lankan university students, with significant impact on mental health and academic performance. Traditional stress assessment methods rely heavily on self-reported questionnaires, which introduce potential biases and cannot provide continuous monitoring.

Our research addresses the unique challenge of code-mixed Sinhala-English communication patterns among Sri Lankan students, where conventional single-language stress detection systems fail to capture stress indicators expressed through bilingual speech patterns.

Recent advances in multimodal AI systems show that combining voice analysis with physiological monitoring achieves 87.3% accuracy in stress classification, significantly outperforming single-modality approaches.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Code-Mixed Language Processing

No existing system can effectively analyze stress indicators in Sinhala-English code-mixed speech patterns common among Sri Lankan students. Current voice-based systems are designed for monolingual populations and fail to capture cultural expressions of stress.

Real-time Physiological Integration

Lack of systems that combine voice analysis with real-time physiological monitoring (heart rate, pulse) for comprehensive stress assessment. Most research relies on single modality approaches with limited accuracy.

Cultural Adaptation

Absence of culturally validated stress assessment tools for Sri Lankan academic contexts. Western-based assessment frameworks overlook unique cultural expressions and academic stressors specific to Sri Lankan university environments.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem


How can we develop a culturally-appropriate, real-time stress assessment system that accurately detects and manages academic stress in bilingual Sri Lankan university students through multimodal AI analysis?

Sri Lankan university students face unique challenges: 71.2% experience moderate to severe academic stress, but existing assessment tools are designed for Western, monolingual populations. Students naturally communicate using Sinhala-English code-mixing, creating a gap where traditional stress detection systems fail to capture culturally-specific stress expressions. Additionally, current methods rely on subjective self-reporting rather than objective, real-time physiological and vocal analysis.


Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

Our integrated multimodal system combines three key innovations:

  • Advanced Voice Analysis: Deep learning models trained to detect acoustic stress markers in code-mixed Sinhala-English speech
  • Real-time Physiological Monitoring: ESP32-based pulse rate sensors providing continuous heart rate data during assessments
  • AI-Powered Personalization: Machine learning algorithms that adapt interventions based on individual stress patterns, cultural context, and physiological responses
The system delivers personalized stress management through music therapy, meditation guidance, interactive games, and virtual assistant support.

Research Objectives

Voice-Based Stress Detection

Develop and validate a sophisticated voice analysis component that detects acoustic stress markers from code-mixed Sinhala-English speech recordings, analyzing prosodic elements, voice quality parameters, and spectral features with 90%+ accuracy using Whisper ASR, Librosa, and TensorFlow/Keras CNN models.

Multimodal Data Integration

Create a comprehensive system that integrates real-time physiological data (heart rate, pulse variability) with linguistic analysis using ESP32 microcontrollers and pulse rate sensors to provide holistic stress assessment, achieving 87%+ classification accuracy across five stress levels.

Cultural Context Integration

Establish a cultural adaptation framework that incorporates Sri Lankan-specific academic stressors, local expressions, and bilingual communication patterns using BERT models and machine learning algorithms to ensure culturally relevant stress assessment.

Personalized Intervention System

Implement AI-driven personalized interventions including adaptive music therapy, guided meditation, interactive stress-relief games, and virtual assistant support based on individual stress profiles and real-time feedback through web and mobile interfaces.

Methodology

Figure 1: Integrated Multimodal Academic Stress Assessment System Architecture

System Processing Flow

1

Data Input

Voice Input: Sri Lankan university students provide Sinhala-English code-mixed speech samples

Physiological Input: Real-time heart rate and BPM monitoring via ESP32 sensors

2

Multi-Modal Processing

Voice Analysis: Whisper ASR, Librosa audio processing, CNN stress classification, and bilingual feature extraction

Physiological Analysis: Real-time heart rate analysis, stress level calculation, and pattern recognition

3

AI Processing Engine

Core Engine: TensorFlow/Keras models, BERT NLP processing, multimodal data fusion

Performance: 87.3% accuracy with cultural context integration and machine learning pipeline

4

Intelligent Output System

Stress Assessment: 5-point Likert scale with 2.8s response time and real-time feedback

Intervention System: Personalized music therapy, guided meditation, interactive games, and coping strategies

Virtual Assistant: 24/7 conversational AI support with BERT-based NLP and bilingual capabilities
